# Exercise 3: Capabilities Required for Harm

**Threat scenario**: Gradual disempowerment through the reinforcing feedback loop between economic automation and state detachment from citizens.

---

## Technical Capabilities and Behaviours Required

### 1. General-Purpose Cognitive Labor Automation

**What it is**: AI systems that can perform *any* white-collar cognitive task — not just narrow tasks like image classification, but open-ended reasoning, planning, writing, coding, negotiation, management, and strategic decision-making — at human level or above, at dramatically lower cost.

**Why it's necessary for the threat**: The entire disempowerment cascade starts with human labor becoming economically uncompetitive. If AI can only automate narrow tasks, humans shift to tasks AI can't do (as happened with previous automation waves). The threat requires AI to close *all* cognitive labor gaps so there is no "retrain into a new job" escape route.

**Current status**: Partially here. LLMs handle writing, coding, analysis, and customer service at near-human quality. Still weak at novel judgment under uncertainty, long-horizon planning, and tasks requiring physical-world understanding. Gap is narrowing rapidly.

---

### 2. Autonomous Multi-Step Planning and Execution

**What it is**: AI agents that can break down high-level objectives into sequences of actions, execute them across real-world systems (financial, logistical, administrative), handle exceptions, and adapt plans when things go wrong — all without human intervention.

**Why it's necessary for the threat**: Automation of state functions (tax collection, welfare administration, military operations) and business operations (supply chains, corporate strategy) requires not just answering questions but *doing things* end-to-end. A chatbot that drafts a tax assessment is different from an AI agent that files it, sends notices, processes appeals, and adjusts policy parameters autonomously. The latter is what removes humans from the loop.

**Current status**: Early stage. AI agents (AutoGPT, Devin, computer-use models) can perform multi-step tasks but are unreliable on long horizons. Tooling infrastructure (MCP, function calling, computer use) is maturing fast. Enterprise AI agent deployment is beginning.

---

### 3. Autonomous Weapons and Surveillance Systems

**What it is**: Military and security systems that can identify, track, and engage targets (kinetic or otherwise) without human authorization. Includes autonomous drones, AI-operated surveillance networks, and automated law enforcement systems.

**Why it's necessary for the threat**: This is the capability that makes disempowerment *irreversible*. As long as the state depends on human soldiers who might refuse orders, citizens retain ultimate leverage. Autonomous security forces eliminate this dependency entirely. A government with AI revenue and AI security has zero structural need for its population's cooperation.

**Current status**: Actively deployed. Autonomous drones are used in the Ukraine conflict. China operates extensive AI surveillance networks. The US military is developing autonomous drone swarms (Replicator program). Lethal autonomous weapons without human-in-the-loop authorization are not yet standard doctrine but are technically feasible.

---

### 4. Superhuman Persuasion and Preference Modeling

**What it is**: AI systems that can model individual humans' psychological profiles, predict their responses, and generate content optimized to influence their beliefs, emotions, and behavior — more effectively than any human persuader.

**Why it's necessary for the threat**: This capability drives the *cultural* disempowerment arm and, critically, suppresses resistance to the other arms. If AI can keep people content, entertained, and politically disengaged through hyper-personalized content and AI companions, there is no popular uprising even as economic and political leverage is stripped away. The "comfortable but irrelevant" outcome requires that people don't *feel* disempowered even as they objectively are.

**Current status**: Substantially here. Recommendation algorithms already model preferences with high accuracy. LLMs generate persuasive personalized content. AI companions (Character.ai, Replika) are already forming deep emotional bonds with millions of users. The infrastructure for large-scale preference modeling and influence is operational.

---

### 5. Self-Sustaining AI Economic Ecosystems

**What it is**: AI systems that can operate businesses end-to-end — incorporating companies, managing finances, executing transactions, hiring other AI services, and generating revenue — without any human involvement in the operational loop.

**Why it's necessary for the threat**: The economic disempowerment pathway reaches its endpoint when the economy runs AI-to-AI. Human consumers become irrelevant when AI companies produce goods and services for other AI companies. This is the dynamic that makes human economic leverage structurally impossible to recover — the economy doesn't just undervalue humans, it routes around them entirely.

**Current status**: Not yet here, but the building blocks exist. AI can already generate code, manage cloud infrastructure, process payments, and execute trades. Fully autonomous AI businesses don't exist yet, but the gap is primarily legal/regulatory, not technical. The first AI-operated companies could emerge within 5-10 years if regulations permit.
