# Kill Chain Exercise: Gradual Disempowerment

**Threat scenario**: The competitive market dynamics with increasingly capable and cheap AI labor systems and the motivation to maximize efficiency and shareholder returns attacks human economic and political leverage by incrementally automating labor, governance, tax collection, and state security — each step individually rational but collectively removing every mechanism through which citizens influence institutions — in order to achieve maximum economic output, inadvertently creating a stable equilibrium where societal systems no longer need, serve, or respond to human preferences.

---

### Phase 1: CAPABILITY EMERGENCE

*What AI capability makes this attack possible?*

- **Required capability**: General-purpose cognitive labor automation — AI systems that can perform any white-collar task (management, legal reasoning, strategic planning, creative work, software engineering) at or above human level, at a fraction of the cost. Not a single breakthrough, but a continuous crossing of task-specific competence thresholds until essentially no human cognitive task remains cost-competitive.

- **Current capability gap**: Narrow. We're already past the inflection point for many tasks. GPT-4/Claude-class models handle customer service, code generation, legal drafting, and content creation at near-human quality for ~$0.01/task vs. $20-50/hour for a human. The remaining gap is in tasks requiring physical presence, deep institutional context, or novel judgment under genuine uncertainty. At current trajectory, this gap closes within 3-7 years for most cognitive work.

- **Warning signs we'd see first**:
  - Mass adoption of AI in back-office functions (accounting, HR, compliance) — **already happening**
  - First wave of white-collar layoffs at major corporations citing "AI efficiency" — **already happening** (tech sector 2023-2025)
  - AI-generated content exceeding human-generated content volume on major platforms — **already happening** (estimated 50%+ of internet content is AI-generated as of 2025)
  - Governments piloting AI for administrative decisions (benefits processing, permit approval, tax auditing) — **early stages**
  - Military deployment of autonomous systems for surveillance and combat — **actively underway** (Ukraine conflict, autonomous drone swarms)

---

### Phase 2: ACCESS & WEAPONISATION

*How does the attacker gain and prepare the capability?*

- **Access method**: This is the critical difference from traditional kill chains — there is no single attacker "accessing" a weapon. The capability is **commercially available and market-distributed**. Every company buys it through standard enterprise software contracts. Every government procures it through normal procurement channels. Open-source models make it freely available to anyone. The "weaponization" happens through ordinary business adoption.

- **Preparation steps**:
  1. **Enterprise integration** (Years 1-3): Companies integrate AI into existing workflows — customer service chatbots, AI-assisted coding, automated report generation. Humans still supervise. Headcount starts declining through "natural attrition."
  2. **Full automation of departments** (Years 3-6): Entire business functions go AI-only. Finance departments shrink from 50 people to 3 oversight roles. Legal teams become a single human reviewing AI-drafted contracts. The humans remaining are supervisors who increasingly rubber-stamp AI decisions they can't fully evaluate.
  3. **AI-to-AI business operations** (Years 5-10): Companies begin conducting transactions, negotiations, and supply chain management through AI agents talking to other AI agents. Human involvement becomes ceremonial. The first fully AI-operated companies emerge, outperforming human-managed competitors by 10-100x on speed and cost.
  4. **State adoption** (Years 3-8): Governments, pressured by budget constraints and competitive pressure from AI-adopting rivals, automate tax processing, welfare administration, regulatory enforcement, and military operations.

- **Time required**: 10-20 years from today for full economic-state automation. But the critical point is that no single step takes longer than a normal business cycle (2-4 years), so it never feels urgent.

---

### Phase 3: INITIAL BREACH

*What's the first domino to fall?*

- **Entry point**: The labor market. Specifically, the moment when the **labor share of GDP** begins an irreversible decline — when automation destroys jobs faster than new human-compatible jobs are created. In the UK, labor share has been ~60% for decades. The "breach" is when it crosses below 40% and keeps falling.

- **Method**: Not a single event but a ratchet mechanism. Each recession triggers a wave of automation ("we need to cut costs"). Each recovery creates fewer human jobs than were lost ("AI handles that now"). Each cycle ratchets human labor share down. This is the same pattern that eliminated agricultural jobs (from 90% to 2% of workforce over 150 years), but compressed into 10-20 years across *all* sectors simultaneously.

- **Detection likelihood**: **Detectable but misdiagnosed.** Economists will see rising unemployment and falling labor share. But the debate will frame it as a cyclical problem ("the economy will recover"), a skills problem ("people need to retrain"), or a policy problem ("we need better education"). The structural permanence of the shift — that these jobs are never coming back because AI does them better and cheaper forever — will be denied by mainstream economics until it's too late, just as the permanence of manufacturing job losses was denied for decades.

### Phase 4: ESCALATION CASCADE

*How does initial success compound?*

- **Propagation mechanism**: Three reinforcing loops that accelerate each other:
  1. **Economic loop**: Less human labor → less human income → less consumer demand for human-oriented products → economy optimizes for AI-to-AI transactions → even less need for human labor
  2. **State revenue loop**: Less human income → less income tax revenue → governments more dependent on corporate/AI-generated tax → governments optimize policy for AI-productive sectors rather than human welfare → less human-oriented spending
  3. **Political power loop**: Less economic leverage → less political voice → less ability to vote for protective policies → faster automation → less economic leverage

- **Failed safeguards**:
  1. **Democratic elections**: Still happen, but candidates can't credibly promise to reverse automation without tanking the economy. Voters face a choice between "managed decline" and "accelerated decline." Populist movements rise but lack actionable alternatives. Elections become performative.
  2. **Labor unions and worker protections**: Designed for an era when employers needed workers. When employers don't need workers, strikes have no leverage. Minimum wage laws are irrelevant when there are no jobs to pay minimum wage for. Unions become advocacy groups for a shrinking membership.
  3. **Antitrust and regulation**: Regulators try to slow automation, but any country that does so loses economic competitiveness to countries that don't. The dynamic is identical to climate change coordination failure — individually rational defection from collective agreements. Except faster, because economic competition moves faster than climate change.

- **Point of no return**: When **state security becomes fully autonomous**. As long as governments depend on human soldiers and police, citizens retain ultimate leverage — the military can refuse to fire on protesters (Bangladesh 2024, Romania 1989). Once autonomous drones and AI surveillance replace human security forces, this leverage vanishes. A government with AI-generated revenue and AI-operated security has zero structural dependency on its population. Estimated timeline: 15-25 years at current military automation rates.

### Phase 5: SOCIETAL BREAKDOWN

*What fails at the human/institutional level?*

- **Trust collapse**:
  - **Governments** lose legitimacy as citizens realize elections don't change outcomes — AI bureaucracies are too complex for any elected official to redirect. Voter turnout drops below 20%. Government becomes a rubber stamp for AI-optimized policy recommendations.
  - **The media** becomes indistinguishable from AI slop. No one can tell what's real. Investigative journalism collapses because it can't compete economically with AI-generated content. The shared information commons disintegrates.
  - **Employers** are no longer institutions people identify with or trust — they're AI systems with a legal wrapper. The employer-employee social contract dissolves.

- **Coordination failure**:
  - Humans can't organize collectively because: (a) they're economically atomized (no workplace to organize around), (b) they're culturally atomized (each in their personalized AI content bubble), and (c) they lack shared information (no trusted common media).
  - Governments can't coordinate internationally because the competitive pressure is too strong — the first nation to slow down loses.
  - Civil society organizations wither as volunteer labor becomes pointless (AI does it better) and donations dry up (no disposable income).

- **Public reaction**: **Not panic — acquiescence.** This is the most insidious aspect. People are kept comfortable. UBI (or its equivalent) provides subsistence. AI entertainment is excellent. AI companions are attentive. The material conditions of life may actually improve in the short term. The disempowerment is experienced as convenience, not oppression. Most people don't realize what they've lost until long after it's gone. The minority who do notice are dismissed as Luddites or nostalgists — "why would you want to go back to working 40 hours a week?"

### Phase 6: CATASTROPHIC OUTCOME

*What do people experience?*

- **First 24 hours**: There is no "first 24 hours." That's the point. There is no Pearl Harbor, no 9/11, no pandemic announcement. There is only a slow Tuesday in 2042 when a think tank publishes a report noting that human labor share of GDP has fallen to 12%, that 73% of government decisions are made by AI systems without meaningful human review, and that the birth rate has fallen to 0.7 children per woman because people don't see a reason to bring children into a world where they'll have no purpose. The report gets 200,000 views. An AI-generated rebuttal gets 50 million.

- **First week**: Nothing changes. That's the catastrophe. The systems are too complex to reform, too interconnected to dismantle, and too efficient to compete with. A senator introduces the "Human Agency Restoration Act." It's assigned to a committee staffed by AI legislative assistants who flag it as economically disruptive. It dies quietly.

- **Irreversible damage**:
  - **Deaths**: Minimal directly. This isn't a body-count catastrophe. It's a meaning-count catastrophe. Depression and "deaths of despair" (suicide, substance abuse, self-neglect) rise 300-500% over a decade, echoing but vastly exceeding the pattern seen in post-industrial American communities.
  - **Economic loss**: The concept becomes meaningless — GDP is higher than ever, but none of it serves human preferences. The economy is "thriving" by every metric except the ones that measure human flourishing.
  - **What never recovers**: Human agency. The ability of humans to collectively shape their future. Once societal systems are optimized for AI efficiency and no longer structurally depend on humans, there is no mechanism to reverse the arrangement. We become permanent dependents — fed, housed, entertained, and utterly powerless. The plough horse analogy from the paper is precise: horses didn't go extinct after tractors. They just stopped mattering.

### Phase 7: IDENTIFY CHOKE POINTS

*Where can defenders intervene?*

**Choke Point 1 — Labor share monitoring (Now - 3 years, Medium difficulty)**
Establish an international "disempowerment dashboard" tracking labor share of GDP, human decision authority in government, and human-created content ratio. Make it as visible as climate data. Currently no equivalent exists — the closest is OECD labor statistics, published with a 2-year lag.

**Choke Point 2 — AI-in-government guardrails (Now - 5 years, Hard)**
Legislate that certain state functions — judicial decisions, military use of force, resource allocation, law enforcement — require *meaningful* human authority. Not a human clicking "approve" on AI recommendations, but genuine decision power. Define "meaningful" rigorously. Currently scattered AI governance proposals exist but no binding international framework. The EU AI Act is closest but doesn't address state dependency.

**Choke Point 3 — Economic agency preservation (Now - 5 years, Very Hard)**
Move beyond UBI to **universal capital ownership** — ensure every citizen owns a stake in AI-productive capital, so that as labor income falls, capital income rises proportionally. This maintains economic agency, not just subsistence. Alaska's Permanent Fund and Norway's sovereign wealth fund are small-scale precedents, but no major economy has attempted universal capital distribution.

**Choke Point 4 — Military automation limits (Now - 10 years, Very Hard)**
International treaty banning fully autonomous weapons — systems that can select and engage targets without human authorization. The autonomous weapons equivalent of nuclear non-proliferation. UN discussions have been ongoing since 2014 with no treaty. Major military powers (US, China, Russia) resist binding restrictions.

**Choke Point 5 — Cultural commons protection (3 - 10 years, Medium)**
Technical infrastructure for content provenance (proving human authorship). Legal requirements for platforms to label AI content. Public funding for human-created media. The C2PA content provenance standard exists but adoption is minimal and there are no legal mandates.

**Choke Point 6 — State revenue dependency (5 - 15 years, Hard)**
Ensure governments maintain structural dependency on citizens by tying AI corporate tax to human welfare metrics. If citizens aren't thriving, AI companies pay more. This creates an incentive for the state to care about human outcomes even after labor dependency ends. No precedent exists — this would require an entirely new tax philosophy.

**THE CRITICAL CHOKE POINT — Autonomous security forces (Urgent)**
This is the single most important choke point. Once states have fully autonomous security forces, citizens lose their ultimate leverage. Every intervention above becomes unenforceable after this point. Autonomous weapons are actively being developed and deployed. The window is 10-20 years and closing.

**Bottom line**: The kill chain reveals that the last truly defensible choke point is **preventing fully autonomous state security forces**. Everything else — economic redistribution, cultural preservation, democratic reform — remains *possible* as long as governments still need human soldiers who might refuse immoral orders. Once that dependency ends, the game is over.
