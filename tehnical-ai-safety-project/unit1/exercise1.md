1a.
Understanding your base

For the paper, codebase or project idea that you’d be excited to build, explain the following in your own words: 

What problem is this work trying to solve, and why does it matter for AI safety?
What approach did the authors take, and why did they make those choices over alternatives?
What are the key assumptions this work rests on? What would change if any of them were wrong?
Aim for <200 words. If you can't explain it simply, you probably don't understand it well enough yet.

1b.
Generate directions

Pick your top 3 most interesting directions for extending this work. For each, write one sentence on why pursuing it could be useful.

You'll practice explaining your reasoning to peers who don't know your project, and they'll test whether your explanation holds up and whether your directions follow from the base work.
