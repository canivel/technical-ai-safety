Unit 3: Update and iterate

Using your results
Every time you get a result, you face a decision:

It confirms your assumption — narrow further, escalate to a more thorough test, or move to the next assumption on your list
It surprises you — widen again. Why was your prediction wrong? Is the assumption flawed, or was the test? This is often where the most interesting findings come from.
It's inconclusive — before abandoning it, you can try escalating to a slightly more expensive test that might give a cleaner signal. If that's still inconclusive, move on.
If your direction is a dead end and you have enough time for another loop, pivot. 

If you don't, start writing up. A clear account of "I tested this direction, here's what I found, here's why it didn't work, and here's what I'd suggest trying instead" is a genuinely useful contribution. Don't treat a dead end as failure — treat it as a finding that saves someone else the same work.

Check with your mentor if you're unsure. They can help you judge whether you have time for another loop or whether your remaining time is better spent writing up what you've learned.
