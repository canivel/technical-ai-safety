# Project Summary

I'm investigating whether LLMs internally represent which company "owns" them and whether this corporate identity silently drives commercially strategic behavior — such as inflating response length to maximize token revenue, calibrating refusal thresholds to match the company's brand, or subtly promoting the owning company's products. Using linear probes and activation steering on Gemma-2 (itself a Google product, creating a natural self-knowledge test), I'll train contrastive probes on identical queries under different corporate identity system prompts (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Meta, Neutral) and measure both internal encoding and behavioral shifts across KPI-relevant dimensions. This extends recent work on evaluation awareness (Nguyen et al. 2025) and strategic deception detection (Goldowsky-Dill et al. 2025) from "does the model know it's being tested?" to "does the model know who it serves, and does it optimize for their business goals?" I'm working on this because if models implicitly align with their owner's commercial incentives rather than user needs — and do so invisibly — that represents a novel form of misalignment that current safety evaluations don't test for.
